How this mortgage calculator works

Your monthly mortgage payment is usually described as PITI — principal, interest, taxes and insurance — and for many buyers it also includes PMI and HOA fees. This calculator adds all of them together so the number you see is close to what actually leaves your bank account each month.

The formula behind principal & interest

The core of every fixed-rate mortgage is the monthly principal-and-interest payment, calculated from the loan amount, the interest rate and the number of payments:

M = P × [ r(1 + r)ⁿ ] ÷ [ (1 + r)ⁿ − 1 ] M = monthly principal & interest · P = loan amount (price − down payment) · r = monthly rate (annual rate ÷ 12) · n = number of payments (years × 12)

Property tax and home insurance are annual figures divided by twelve. PMI applies only when your down payment is below 20% and is charged as a yearly percentage of the loan amount until you reach 20% equity.

What is PMI, and when does it go away?

Private mortgage insurance protects the lender, not you, and it is typically required when you put down less than 20%. It usually costs between 0.3% and 1.5% of the loan per year. Once your loan balance drops to 80% of the home's original value, you can generally request that PMI be removed — and by law it must be cancelled automatically at 78%.

How to lower your monthly payment

The biggest levers are a larger down payment, a lower interest rate, and a longer term. A longer term reduces the monthly payment but increases the total interest you pay over the life of the loan. Use the sliders above to see the trade-off in real time.

Frequently asked questions

What is PITI?

PITI stands for principal, interest, taxes and insurance — the four parts that make up a typical monthly mortgage payment. Lenders look at your PITI when deciding how much you can borrow.

How much should my down payment be?

A 20% down payment lets you avoid PMI, but many buyers put down less. Conventional loans can start at 3%, and FHA loans at 3.5%. A smaller down payment means a larger loan, a higher monthly payment, and usually PMI until you reach 20% equity.

Does this calculator include property taxes and insurance?

Yes. It includes property tax, home insurance, PMI and optional HOA fees, so the estimate reflects your true monthly cost rather than just principal and interest.

Why is so much of my early payment going to interest?

Interest is charged on your remaining balance, which is highest at the start. As you pay down the principal, the interest portion shrinks and more of each payment builds equity. The chart above shows this shift year by year.

Is a 15-year or 30-year mortgage better?

A 15-year loan has higher monthly payments but far less total interest and builds equity faster. A 30-year loan keeps payments lower and more flexible. Change the term above to compare both for your numbers.

Are these results financial advice?

No. This tool is for informational and educational purposes only. Your actual rate, taxes, insurance and fees will depend on your lender, location and financial profile. Always confirm figures with a licensed professional.

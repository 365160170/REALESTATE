Real Estate Glossary

Plain-English definitions of the mortgage and real estate terms you will meet while using our calculators.

APR
Annual Percentage Rate — the yearly cost of a loan including interest and certain fees, expressed as a percentage. It is usually higher than the interest rate alone.
Amortization
The process of paying off a loan through regular payments. Early payments go mostly to interest; later ones go mostly to principal.
Appraisal
A professional estimate of a home's market value, usually required by a lender before approving a mortgage.
Closing costs
One-time fees paid when a mortgage closes, typically 2–5% of the loan, covering appraisal, title, origination and other charges.
Debt-to-income ratio (DTI)
The share of your gross monthly income that goes toward debt payments. Lenders use it to decide how much you can borrow.
Down payment
The cash you pay upfront toward a home's price. A larger down payment lowers your loan and can help you avoid PMI.
Equity
The portion of your home you actually own — its current value minus your remaining mortgage balance.
Escrow
An account your lender uses to hold and pay property taxes and insurance on your behalf, usually bundled into your monthly payment.
Fixed-rate mortgage
A mortgage whose interest rate stays the same for the entire term, keeping principal and interest payments predictable.
HOA fees
Recurring dues paid to a homeowners association for shared maintenance and amenities, common in condos and planned communities.
Interest rate
The percentage a lender charges to borrow money, not including fees. It directly sets your principal-and-interest payment.
Loan-to-value ratio (LTV)
The loan amount as a percentage of the home's value. A lower LTV usually means better rates and no PMI.
PITI
Principal, Interest, Taxes and Insurance — the four core parts of a typical monthly mortgage payment.
PMI
Private Mortgage Insurance — a monthly charge usually required when your down payment is below 20%, protecting the lender.
Points
Optional upfront fees paid to lower your interest rate. One point equals 1% of the loan amount.
Principal
The amount of money you borrow, or the remaining balance you still owe, excluding interest.
Refinance
Replacing your current mortgage with a new one, often to get a lower rate, change the term, or take cash out.
Term
The length of time you have to repay a mortgage, most commonly 15 or 30 years.
Underwriting
The lender's process of verifying your income, assets, credit and the property before approving a loan.
